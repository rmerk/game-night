Mahjong Night

Design Direction Exploration

Four layout approaches for Mahjong Night, all sharing the same visual foundation: warm modern elegance with felt teal, cream chrome, and brushed gold. Each direction explores different trade-offs in information density, social prominence, and NMJL card accessibility.

iPad-first (1024px) Felt + Chrome + Gold 44px min tap targets Three Moods
Arriving

Playing
Felt teal dominates. Gold is brass. Tiles and actions prominent. "Focus and flow."
Lingering
Soft cream returns. Gold is muted. Scores and conversation prominent. "One more game?"

Recommendation

Direction A (Balanced Panoramic) is the strongest starting point. It's the reference layout from our spatial specification, provides always-visible NMJL access (critical for players who reference the card constantly), maintains video at seat positions for social presence, and adapts cleanly to mobile.

Elements worth borrowing from other directions:

  • From B: The collapsible sidebar as escape valve — when a player wants maximum felt immersion, they can collapse the NMJL sidebar and access it via overlay instead.
  • From C: The persistent reaction bar concept — reactions should be always-visible near the rack area, not behind a panel. Chat remains a slide-up panel.
  • From B: Larger video frames when sidebar is collapsed — the freed space goes to video and table, not whitespace.

Direction D is explicitly excluded — the chrome header and avatar-only seats contradict the "social first, game second" principle and the "game night" feel that defines Mahjong Night.
